### Problem 9

This problem is worth 10%.

By the Master Theorem, $T(n)=7T(n/2)+n^2$ gives $T(n)=\Theta(n^{\log_2 7}) \approx \Theta(n^{2.807})$. For $T'(n)=aT'(n/4)+n^2$, we need $\log_4 a < 2$ (i.e., $a < 16$) so that $T'(n)=\Theta(n^2)$, which is faster than $n^{2.807}$. If $\log_4 a = 2$ (i.e., $a=16$), then $T'(n)=\Theta(n^2 \log n)$, still faster. If $a > 16$, compare $\log_4 a$ with $\log_2 7 \approx 2.807$: $\log_4 a < 2.807$ means $a < 4^{2.807} \approx 48.5$, so the largest integer $a$ is $48$.
- Key concept: applying the Master Theorem to compare asymptotic growth rates of two divide-and-conquer recurrences.
